Hello!

My name is Jianing Li (李嘉宁), a theoretical nuclear physics postdoctoral researcher at Central China Normal University specializing in QuantumChromodynamics (QCD) matter. My research focuses on understanding the origins of mass and spin in the visible matter of our universe, probing the fundamentals that govern the interactions of quarks and gluons.

I'm particularly interested in the study of QCD phase transitions, transport phenomena, and hydrodynamics, with an emphasis on their roles in heavy-ion and electron-ion collisions. I aim to explore the complex behavior of QCD matter under extreme conditions and reveal the origin of the mass and spin of the visible matter in our universe.

Additionally, I am passionate about connecting these high-energy phenomena to experiments from few-and-many-body systems, such as cold atoms and condensed matter systems, under extreme conditions.

I am excited to continue exploring the intersection of theory and experiment to unravel some of the most profound questions about the nature of matter, the universe, and the forces that shape them.

Research Interests:

  • Hot QCD Matter: QCD phase transition, Transport and hydrodynamic, Phenomenology in heavy-ion collision
  • Cold QCD Matter: Parton distribution, Neutron star, Phenomenology in electron-ion collision
  • Many-body System: Cold atom, Off-equilibrium phenomenon
